Understanding Star Math
A quick guide to the numbers in this report
Scaled Score (SS)
A score on a fixed scale that measures math achievement. Ranges from 600–1400. Higher is better. Allows comparison across grade levels and over time.
Percentile Rank (PR)
Shows how a student compares to peers nationally. PR 50 means the student scored higher than 50% of students in the same grade nationwide.

Insights
What’s Working Well
Pockets of strength across the school

● Kindergarten Excellence

71% at/above benchmark with zero urgent interventions. The only class exceeding the national PR average. Whatever foundational instruction strategies are being used here should be studied and shared with other grades.

● Grade 6 Leads Upper School

68% at benchmark, mean PR of 54 (above national average), zero urgent interventions. Smallest class size (19) may contribute. This class shows that AZIA students can compete nationally when conditions are right.

● Exceptional Individual Talent

Several students perform at elite levels: PR 99, 98, 96, 95, 93 across multiple grades. This demonstrates the school’s capacity to develop high-achieving math students when the conditions are right.

● Upper Grades Outperform

Grades 5, 6, and 7 all have mean PR above 39 and 42–68% at benchmark. The school’s math program appears to strengthen as students progress, suggesting the cumulative effect of instruction is positive.

Support Framework: Where Does Each Class Fall?
A tiered support model based on each class’s data profile
Maintain & Enrich
Grade K & Grade 6
>50% at benchmark, <20% urgent+intervention.
Action: Protect what’s working. Add enrichment for top students. Monitor the small below-benchmark group closely.
Strengthen + Targeted Support
Grades 2, 3, 5, 7
30–50% at benchmark, moderate intervention needs.
Action: Adjust core instruction pacing. Add small-group supports for intervention-band students. Progress-check monthly.
Highest Priority — Intensive Intervention
Grade 1 & Grade 4
<35% at benchmark, >40% in urgent+intervention. Class average is below the benchmark cutoff.
Action: Increase daily protected math instruction time. Add intensive small-group supports (Tier 2/3). Re-teach prerequisite skills while maintaining grade-level exposure. Progress-check weekly or biweekly.
This aligns with MTSS (Multi-Tiered System of Supports) best practices: strengthen core instruction first, then layer targeted interventions, with evidence-based progress monitoring at each tier.

Recommendations
Recommended Actions
Data-informed priorities for administration and staff — from performance, domain mastery, and growth data
  • 1
    Investigate the K-to-1 Drop: The 31-point PR collapse from Kindergarten (51) to Grade 1 (20) is the single most alarming finding. Conduct a curriculum and instructional strategy review for this transition. Consider whether pacing, materials, or differentiation practices differ significantly.
  • 2
    Prioritize Grade 1 & Grade 4 Resources: These two classes account for 45% of all intervention-level students. Consider additional instructional aides, smaller pull-out groups, or dedicated intervention blocks specifically for these grades.
  • 3
    Target the On Watch Cohort in Grades 5 & 7: Thirteen students across these two grades are close to benchmark. Focused small-group instruction or after-school tutoring for this cohort could move the school’s overall benchmark rate above 50%.
  • 4
    Study What Works in K & Grade 6: These classes achieve 71% and 68% at benchmark respectively. Document their instructional practices, grouping strategies, and pacing — then create a playbook that other grades can adapt.
  • 5
    Monitor Class Size Impact: Grade 6 (19 students, 68% at benchmark) and Grade 4 (31 students, 32% at benchmark) suggest class size may be a factor. Evaluate whether redistributing resources to reduce the largest class sizes is feasible.
  • 6
    Launch a School-Wide Geometry Initiative: Geometry is below 44% mastery in every grade. Consider dedicated geometry blocks, hands-on manipulatives, visual/spatial reasoning activities, and professional development focused specifically on geometry instruction.
  • 7
    Strengthen Fraction Foundations in Grades 3–5: Fraction mastery (23–38%) is critically low and directly impacts algebra readiness. Prioritize fraction concepts using visual models (number lines, area models) before moving to procedures.
  • 8
    Shift Toward Conceptual Instruction: The pattern of weak conceptual domains (geometry, fractions, algebraic thinking) alongside relatively stronger procedural domains suggests a need to rebalance instruction toward deeper understanding.
  • 9
    Use Projected Growth Data to Set Targets: Renaissance projects 10–14 point gains by year-end. Classes where actual growth exceeds projections should be celebrated. Classes falling short of projections need mid-year intervention adjustments.
  • 10
    Set a School Goal Metric: Adopt At/Above Benchmark % as the primary outcome metric for this cycle, with Urgent Intervention % as a secondary watchpoint. Track these quarterly with the same Star Math assessment to measure progress.
